## 2. What Was Accomplished

### Faction-aware template system
- **`data/joinable_npc_templates.json`**: 11 → 18 templates. Added 7 new faction-specific templates:
  - `iron_pact_guard` (Iron Accord, L4, requires rep 5)
  - `hollow_warden` (Hollow Covenant, L4, requires rep 5)
  - `ash_serpent_raider` (Ash Serpents, L3, biome: Ash/Scorched/Glass)
  - `veil_warden` (Veilwardens, L4, biome: Corpse/Stormspire)
  - `neon_choir_techie` (Neon Choir, L5, biome: Neon Bogs/Dead City)
  - `bone_circuit_broker` (Bone Circuit, L6, biome: Rust/Dead City)
  - `caravan_guard` (Last Caravans, L3)
- Each has `preferred_factions: ["<Faction Name>"]` (null = universal).

### Per-faction themes
- **`_faction_themes`** in templates JSON: per-faction flavor (name_prefix, race_pref). 10 factions, each with their own prefix ("Iron", "Hollow", "Ash", "Veil", "Neon", "Dust", "Bone", "Ledger", "Caravan", "Echo"). Faction theme OVERRIDES biome theme for the NPC's name prefix and race origin.

### Bi-axial template roll (`_roll_template_for_settlement`)
- Categorizes templates into 4 buckets:
  - `match_both`: preferred_biomes matches AND preferred_factions matches (4x weight)
  - `match_faction_only`: faction matches (3x weight)
  - `match_biome_only`: biome matches (2x weight)
  - `universal` / no-match: (1x weight)
- Faction matches get a stronger boost than biome matches because "settlements belong to a specific faction". This is the weight balancing the user requested.

### `spawn_for_settlement(hex_key, biome, faction, town_size)`
- Generates 1-3 NPCs per town (small/medium/large).
- Deterministic via `_hash_hex_key(hex_key + "|" + biome + "|" + faction)` seed. Same settlement always produces the same NPCs.
- Adds the spawned NPCs to `available_npcs` with `settlement_resident: true` and `settlement_faction: <Faction>`.

### `clear_settlement_residents(hex_key)`
- Removes only the residents for the given hex. Phase 3 test NPCs and hex-spawned NPCs are preserved.
- Called at the start of `_resolve_resident_npcs` so re-entering a settlement doesn't accumulate duplicates.

### Wiring
- **`scripts/WorldGenerator.gd`**: town data now includes `biome: <Biome Name>` (read from the picked tile's biome). Needed for `_resolve_resident_npcs` to know the settlement's biome.
- **`scripts/Settlement.gd`**: `_resolve_resident_npcs` now calls `clear_settlement_residents(hex)` then `spawn_for_settlement(hex, biome, faction, size)`. Replaces the Phase 3 placeholder that picked from a global pool.

### NPC attributes (faction-flavored)
- Name prefix = `_faction_themes[faction].name_prefix` (overrides biome theme).
- Race origin = `_faction_themes[faction].race_pref` (overrides biome theme).
- Role title = `_biome_themes[biome].title` (biome wins for the role flavor, e.g., "bogs-runner" in Neon Bogs).
- The NPC's `faction` field stores the settlement's faction.
- The NPC's `biome` field stores the settlement's biome.

## 3. Key Decisions & Rationale

- **Faction weighting > biome weighting (3x vs 2x):** Settlements are owned by a faction, so the faction identity is stronger than the biome identity. An Iron Accord town in the Ash Wastes still gets Iron-themed NPCs (their faction is "Iron Accord" more strongly than "Ash Wastes"). The 4x bonus for templates matching BOTH reflects the strongest flavor match.
- **Faction theme overrides biome theme for name/race:** The settlement's faction is the "owner" identity, the biome is just the location. An NPC's name should reflect their faction ("Iron Kira" not "Ash Kira") because they identify with their faction more than their biome.
- **Biome theme still wins for the role title:** "bogs-runner" is a role specific to the Bogs biome. Even in an Iron Accord town in the Bogs, the role is "bogs-runner" because that's the activity they do. So role = biome, name/race = faction.
- **Templates with `preferred_biomes = null` and `preferred_factions = null` are "universal":** They can spawn in any settlement regardless of biome or faction. wanderer_common is the most prominent example.
- **Templates with a preference that doesn't match the settlement are still eligible** (treated as universal, 1x weight): e.g., wasteland_scavenger in a Hollow Covenant town. It still has a chance to spawn, but a lower one.
- **`clear_settlement_residents(hex_key)` vs clear all:** Clearing by hex_key prevents losing NPCs from other settlements if the player visits multiple in sequence.

## 7. Context That Must Survive

- **`spawn_for_settlement(hex_key, biome, faction, town_size)` is the v0.7.0 API.** Replaces the Phase 3 placeholder pool selection. Deterministic via hex-key hash.
- **`clear_settlement_residents(hex_key)`** is called by `_resolve_resident_npcs` to prevent duplicate accumulation on re-enter. It only clears NPCs flagged with `settlement_resident: true` AND matching the hex_key. Phase 3 test NPCs (no `settlement_resident` flag) and hex-spawned NPCs from `spawn_for_hex` (different flag) are left alone.
- **The template roll weighting** is: match_both 4x, match_faction_only 3x, match_biome_only 2x, universal 1x. Documented in the function's docstring.
- **Templates need `preferred_factions: null` OR include the faction.** Templates without `preferred_factions` are universal across factions.
- **NPC name priority:** faction theme > biome theme for `name_prefix` and `race_pref`. Biome theme wins for the role title (the activity they do is biome-specific).
- **The `seed()` call inside `spawn_for_settlement` is intentional:** makes the whole generation loop deterministic per settlement. Other systems that use `randf()` after this will see the same RNG state — `seed()` was called for the whole sequence, not per roll.

## 10. (Remedy's note)

The user's reminder — "settlements belong to a specific faction, the world needs to balance the weights between settlements to faction ratio" — was the right push. Without it, v0.7.0 would have been a simpler biome-only spawn that ignored factions. The faction awareness makes the world feel more alive: Iron Accord towns get Iron-themed NPCs, Hollow Covenant towns get Hollow-themed NPCs, and the weighting (3x for faction match vs 1x for universal) ensures the spawn ratio reflects the world's faction distribution.

The "faction theme overrides biome theme for name/race" decision was the subtle one. Both themes have a `name_prefix` and `race_pref`, and the temptation was to just blend them or pick biome. But faction identity is stronger than biome identity — a player visiting their first Iron Accord town should immediately recognize "oh, these are Iron people" via the names ("Iron Kira", "Iron Marda"). The biome flavor comes through in the role title ("bogs-runner") and the template selection itself.
