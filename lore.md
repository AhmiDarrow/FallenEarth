# Fallen Earth Lore

**Earth IV — A Grim Post-Apocalyptic World of Rifts, Runners, and the Underearth**

---

## The Cataclysm

Earth IV was colonized in 2035. What began as corporate water wars between powerful companies quickly escalated into full-scale climate wars. These conflicts literally **broke open the Underearth** — a dark, ancient realm beneath (or between) the surface world.

From the fissure poured ancient horrors, monsters, beasts, and eldritch entities. The wars that followed with these underworld threats finalized the collapse of the old civilization. A few hundred years have passed since the Fall.

The old world ended in fire, flood, and the awakening of what had been buried.

---

## The World Today

The surface is a brutal mix of wasteland and encroaching darkness. Sparse outposts and settlements cling to existence. The Underearth continues to intrude, slowly swallowing more territory through unstable **rifts**.

**Key Facts:**
- Pre-collapse technology was roughly modern-day level.
- The most advanced and unstable technology now originates from the Underearth itself.
- High-tech is rare, scavenged, often grafted onto rugged "Mad Max" vehicles and gear.
- Cybernetics are common but frequently glitchy or dangerous.
- The world feels grim and brutal, yet strangely beautiful — stylish cyberpunk decay mixed with cosmic/eldritch horror (Shadowrun × Cthulhu vibes).

---

## Riftspire

The only true megacity on Earth IV. Built directly around the original fissure into the Underearth, Riftspire is the sole place where **Upworlders** and **Underworlders** regularly mingle in large numbers.

It is a sprawling, dangerous, neon-lit sprawl of rusted towers, glowing fissures, black markets, faction strongholds, and desperate opportunity. Control of its districts is split between the major powers.

Riftspire is both the heart of civilization and its most volatile pressure point.

## Races

Players choose an **origin** (Upworld or Underworld) and a specific **race**. Each race provides low base D&D-style stats (STR/DEX/CON/INT/WIS/CHA) reflecting their adaptation. Classes add small mods.

### Upworlders (Surface Origin)

| Race          | Description                                      | Stat Tilt                          | Flavor |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------|--------|
| **Human**     | Baseline survivors from the surface cities.      | Balanced stats                     | Jack-of-all-trades with no severe weaknesses. Neutral faction standing. |
| **Mutant**    | Radiation-scarred adaptors hardened by the wastes. | +Health, +RAD resist, −Hunger cap | Tough but constantly hungry. |
| **Sentient AI** | Conscious machine minds housed in synthetic bodies. | +Energy (stamina), −Health       | High stamina, fragile frame. No food drain bonus. |
| **Cyborg**    | Chrome grafted onto flesh.                       | +Health, −Energy                   | Durable frame with power-hungry implants. Tech synergy. |

### Underworlders (Underearth Origin)

These races originate from or have been heavily changed by the Underearth.

| Race        | Description                                              | Stat Tilt                          | Flavor |
|-------------|----------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------|--------|
| **Chthon**  | Pale, elongated humanoids adapted to darkness. Many have glowing veins and subtle mutations. | +Hunger resistance, +RAD resist, −Energy | Closest to “surface humans” but deeply changed by the Underearth. |
| **Vesperid**| Insectoid-human hybrids with chitin plating and compound eyes. | +Health, +RAD resist, −Hunger    | Often seen as eerie or monstrous by Upworlders. Extremely durable. |
| **Nullborn**| Beings touched by the void between realities. Their forms flicker; unnatural calm. | +Energy, +RAD resist, −Health    | Extremely rare. Some say they are partially unmade. |
| **Revenant**| Reanimated corpses kept alive by Underearth tech or parasites. | +Health, +Hunger resist, −Energy | Not truly undead — more like “biologically repurposed.” Hard to kill, costly to sustain. |

## Major Factions

### The Iron Accord (Primary Upworld / Human Power)
A powerful coalition of old-world corporations and warlords that survived the collapse. They control most of the surface’s remaining heavy industry and mercenary forces. They view the Underearth as a resource to be exploited rather than feared.

**Motto:** “Order from the ashes.”

### The Hollow Covenant (Primary Underworld Power)
A powerful theocratic-technocratic alliance of Chthon, Vesperid, and Nullborn leaders. They believe the Underearth is not a curse, but a gift — and that humanity’s future lies below. They control much of Riftspire’s lower districts and many underworld cults.

**Motto:** “We are the children of what came before.”

---

## Independent & Neutral Factions

### Independent Factions
- **The Ash Serpents** — Ruthless surface raiders and smugglers who specialize in deep Underearth runs. Known for using modified beasts as mounts.
- **The Veilwardens** — Secretive cult obsessed with recovering forbidden knowledge from the Underearth. Many members are Nullborn.
- **The Neon Choir** — Tech cult that worships and modifies unstable AI and cybernetics. Extremely fashionable in Riftspire’s underworld clubs.
- **The Dust Parliament** — Loose alliance of independent settlement leaders attempting to remain neutral between the Iron Accord and Hollow Covenant.
- **The Bone Circuit** — Controls much of Riftspire’s organ, cybernetic, and monster-part black market trade. Extremely dangerous and well-connected.

### Neutral Factions (Third Options)
- **The Black Ledger** — Network of spies, hackers, and information brokers who sell secrets to the highest bidder. Operate across Riftspire and surface outposts. Motivation: profit and survival.
- **The Last Caravans** — Massive convoys of heavily armed nomadic traders who travel between settlements. Fiercely protect their neutrality. Hate both major powers equally.
- **The Echo Wardens** — Small but respected archivists and historians dedicated to preserving pre-collapse knowledge. Maintain hidden archives and are generally left alone.

## The Rift Runners

Rift Runners are those crazy (or desperate) enough to regularly delve into the darkness of the Underearth. The title carries both respect and stigma.

Runners leave the relative safety of Riftspire or settlements for personal reasons: riches, power, adventure, revenge, curiosity, or the simple need to survive. Each runner’s motivation is their own.

Runners are hired by factions (and occasionally even the Iron Accord) to enter unstable rifts, retrieve valuable materials, collapse threats, and return with whatever the depths offer.

---

## Player Background

At the start of the game, the player has just been released (or escaped) from a **workcamp** to which they were sold as a youth. They arrive in Riftspire (or a small outpost) with almost nothing.

They are on their own. Their immediate goal is survival and carving out a future in a world that has already taken so much.

Over time, players claim land and begin building their own settlement, centered around rift-running operations. The settlement can grow from a small camp into a functional town with recruited NPCs providing new services.

---

## Technology & The Soul Link

Every person carries a **Soul Link** — an implanted AI chip that allows interaction with technology, especially the strange cyber-organic life of the Underearth. This link is what enables taming and certain advanced interactions.

Advanced technology is unstable and heavily scavenged. Vehicles and equipment blend crude salvage with grafted high-tech components. Drones, AI, and cybernetics can be both tools and sources of horror.

---

## The Rifts and Runs

**Rifts** are temporary entrances to the Underearth that appear naturally due to underworld intrusion. Most only stay open for a limited time (a few in-game days). The Iron Accord actively tries to seal the largest and most dangerous ones using sentient digging machines.

Smaller or less threatening rifts are often ignored — this is where Rift Runners thrive.

**A Run** begins when a player enters a rift. Inside are dangerous creatures, ancient technology, resources, **lore scrolls** (which can grant small permanent stat bonuses or be sold as high-value commodities), and rare crafting recipes.

Completing the objectives inside a rift causes it to collapse. Some major rifts are hand-crafted and may reveal deeper truths about the Underearth.

---

## Taming and Companions

Rift Runners can tame wild cybernetic creatures using special **Elemental Fruits** after defeating them in combat. The fruit determines the creature’s elemental affinity and abilities. Tamed creatures become loyal companions attuned to the runner’s Soul Link.

### The Four Elemental Fruits

| Fruit       | Element   | Combat Bonus                    | Mount Bonus                          |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Ashfruit**   | Fire      | Increased damage                | Faster movement speed                |
| **Voidbloom**  | Void      | Chance to inflict fear          | Short-phase teleport                 |
| **Ironroot**   | Earth     | Higher health and defense       | Carry extra inventory weight         |
| **Stormgourd** | Lightning | Chance to stun on hit           | Faster attack speed while mounted    |

Tames can level up through combat and exploration. Some powerful tames can pull cargo containers for trading or megasleds. Both Upworlders and Underworlders use and breed these creatures.

## Settlements & Base Building

Players eventually establish their own settlement. Building is free-form placement inspired by Rust and Valheim — walls, floors, workbenches, storage, defenses, etc. Materials are crafted or scavenged.

**NPC Recruitment:**
- Faction NPCs (and some neutrals) can be recruited to the player’s base.
- Recruitment requires sufficient **reputation** with their faction plus player level.
- Once recruited, they function as **storefronts / vendors**, selling faction-specific goods, services, and upgrades.
- A player’s settlement can grow into a real, functional hub with multiple services.

## Biomes of the Surface

The overworld features ten distinct biomes with varying danger, resources, and rift frequency:

- **Ash Wastes** — Barren, toxic dust plains (common starter).
- **Rust Canyons** — Deep canyons filled with rusted wreckage (high rift chance).
- **Neon Bogs** — Polluted wetlands with glowing flora.
- **Scorched Plains** — Cracked earth and constant heat (low rift chance).
- **Ironwood Thicket** — Dense metallic trees and vines.
- **Glass Dunes** — Shimmering sand made of melted glass (high rift chance).
- **Corpse Fields** — Old battlegrounds littered with bones and wreckage.
- **Stormspire Highlands** — High plateaus with constant lightning (very high rift chance).
- **Toxin Marshes** — Heavily polluted swamps.
- **Dead City Outskirts** — Ruined megacity edges — extremely dangerous.

Biomes influence resource types, movement, and the likelihood of rifts appearing.

## Creatures of the Surface

The surface hosts both neutral and aggressive wildlife, many of which are cyber-organic hybrids adapted to the post-Fall world.

**Neutral examples:**
- Ashveil Grazer — Slow fungal herd beast.
- Lumen Drifter — Bioluminescent floating jelly that provides light.
- Rustcarapace Scuttler — Armored scrap cleaner.
- Silkroot Tapper — Rooted resin producer.
- Echo Chorister — Singing burrow colony.

**Aggressive examples:**
- Charnel Stalker — Pack-hunting quadruped predator with tendrils.
- Voidspine Leech — Parasitic energy drainer.
- Mycelial Behemoth — Massive spore-hurling hulk.
- Glimmer Swarm — Coordinated acidic insects.
- Ferroclaw Reaver — Ferro-organic horror with magnetic and drilling traits.

Deeper within rifts, even stranger and more dangerous entities await.

## Dynamic Threat System (Design Note)

Rift frequency and intensity scales with player settlement expansion. The more you build and stabilize, the more aggressively the Underearth pushes back. Creates natural tension between expansion and survival — there's no "safe" endgame state. Only deeper into the dark.

---

## Player Story Delivery Method

The workcamp backstory should be revealed through **rift lore discoveries** (scrolls, audio logs, environmental storytelling inside rifts). No exposition dumps. Let the player piece together what happened by exploring the very horrors that were caused by corporate exploitation.

---

## Elemental Fruit Choice Impact

The FIRST elemental fruit a player chooses should meaningfully shift their early-game play pattern:
- **Fire (Ashfruit)** → Direct combat focus, damage scaling from day one
- **Void (Voidbloom)** → Stealth/utility route — fear effects, teleport escape for exploration
- **Earth (Ironroot)** → Tank/gather route — defense and weight advantages for heavy looting
- **Lightning (Stormgourd)** → Speed/Agility route — attack speed and movement focus

---

## Tone & Themes

Fallen Earth is about survival, ambition, and carving meaning from ruin.

Core themes that emerge through play:
- Personal freedom and revenge after exploitation (the workcamp background).
- The tension between exploiting the Underearth and respecting its power.
- Building something lasting in a world that wants to swallow it.
- The blurred line between man, machine, and monster.
- Discovering the true nature of the Underearth.

The world is cynical and dangerous, but not without moments of strange beauty, loyalty, and hard-won hope.
